Learn to play: 40k

Warhammer 40K is a tabletop game played with miniatures. It includes a complex and intricate background story, massive boards, and deep tactical gameplay. However, warhammer has been great in making guides like the video above.

Age of Sigmar How to Play

The videos below will guide you through setting up and playing your first games of Warhammer Age of Sigmar. You’ll see what’s on your units’ warscrolls and how each phase of the game works. You’ll go from your first turn in a small skirmish to finishing epic clashes in no time!

How to Play: Warcry

Warcry is a skirmish game for 2 or more players set in the Mortal Realm, Warhammer Age of Sigmar iconic setting. This a Warcry Beginners Guide if you are completely new to the game. Feel free to refer you friends to this guide, if you are trying to get them into the game (and they have no clue what you are talking about).Warcry is easy to start playing, fast and brutal. Traditionally it is played between 2 players but browsing online the rules are easily adapted for solo games.

How to Play: Underworlds

In Warhammer Underworlds, victory is secured by scoring the most glory points! How you gain these is up to you and there are several strategic options. Maybe you’ll slay your enemies in brutal combat. Or perhaps you’ll build a deck that rewards you for careful positioning and holding objectives – the choice is yours.

How to Play: Kill-Team

The Kill Team rules set shares a lot of DNA with its big brother Warhammer 40,000. Both are looking at combat in the 41st Millennium and share a lot of core systems and general principles, with differences emerging in scale. Warhammer 40,000 is about grand plans, sweeping manoeuvres and the clash of massive armies, while Kill Team is about moment-to-moment action.
